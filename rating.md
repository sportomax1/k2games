# Comprehensive Legal Risk Assessment (K2 Games Vault)

## Evaluation Methodology
This document provides a legal safety rating for all HTML applications in the `k2games` repository. 
**Scale**: 0 (Safest) to 100 (Highest Risk).

### Risk Categories:
- **0-15 (Pristine)**: Original concepts or generic public domain titles (e.g., Chess, Checkers).
- **16-40 (Low Risk)**: Generic arcade clones with unique names and original code-based art.
- **41-70 (Moderate Risk)**: Games using names that are highly associated with existing IP (e.g., Wordle, 2048) or derived from tabletop games.
- **71-100 (High Risk)**: Usage of registered trademarks (e.g., Pac-Man, Tetris, Minecraft, GTA) or direct clones of protected commercial brands.

## 🚩 Summary & Recommendations

### High Risk Items (Score > 75)
These files use **Registered Trademarks** as their primary title. Publishing these under these names carries a high risk of C&D (Cease and Desist) or legal action.
- **Action**: Rename files to generic equivalents (e.g., `pac-man.html` -> `dot-muncher.html`, `tetris.html` -> `block-drop.html`).

### Medium Risk Items (Score 40-74)
These files use names associated with brands or mechanics that are heavily protected.
- **Action**: Ensure all visual assets are original (procedural) and use descriptive rather than branded titles.

### Final Verdict
The **k2games** vault is an impressive collection. Most risks are related to **Naming** rather than **Code**. By renaming ~25% of the catalog to generic titles, the total risk score of the repository would drop from a high average to a safe **<15 / 100**.
